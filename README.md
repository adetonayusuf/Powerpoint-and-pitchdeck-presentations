### Why Presentations Matter

Great presentations matter because:

	Humans respond strongly to visuals and structure.
	A well-designed deck influences decision-making.
	Different audiences require different storytelling styles.
	Professional formatting signals credibility and confidence.
	Clear, structured slides make technical financial content easy to digest.

A powerful pitchbook blends:

	Design + Structure + Logic + Insight + Simplicity

### Types of Professional Presentations
1. Distributed Presentations (Designed to be Read)

		Text-heavy
		Analytical
		Used for investment banking pitchbooks, project reports & board packs

2. Live / Delivered Presentations

		Visual storytelling
		Minimal text
		Ideal for sales briefs, investor pitches & executive communication
		Animations & transitions used effectively

 ### Core Structure of a Professional Pitchbook

A world-class pitch deck typically includes:
1. Title Page
- Branding, presenter details, date, confidentiality note.

2. Table of Content
- Clear navigation and expected flow.

3. Executive Summary
- Key messages, strategic insights, high-level conclusions.

4. Company Overview
- Background
- Products/services
- Market presence & positioning

5. Business Model
- Revenue streams
- Cost structure
- Unit economics and growth levers

6. Operational & Financial Forecast
- Revenue forecast
- Cost projections
- KPI metrics
- Scenario/sensitivity analysis

7. Shareholder & Liquidity Analysis
- Shareholder breakdown
- Liquidity metrics
- Capital structure

8. Industry Analysis
- Market trends
- Competitive landscape
- Corporate finance activity (M&A, IPOs, fundraising)

9. Valuation
- Historical share price performance
- Comparable company analysis
- Precedent transactions
- Valuation summary
- DCF
- Sensitivity tables

10. Strategic Recommendations / Deal Opportunities
- Expansion opportunities
- M&A options
- Strategic initiatives

11. Team Overview
- Deal team
- Track record
- Tombstone page

12. Appendices
- Supporting charts
- Detailed financial tables
- Methodology

- Home: formatting, alignment, quick tools

## Case Study: Investment Banking Pitchbook Cleanup
### Scenario
A junior analyst submits a raw, unformatted deck.

### Actions Taken
- Determine presentation purpose (print vs. live).
- Set slide dimensions (4:3 for printing, 16:9 for live).
- Build a custom slide master with brand rules.
- Apply theme colors & fonts.
- Insert layout guides for alignment.
- Rebuild charts and tables professionally.
- Clean formatting (margins, spacing, font hierarchy).
- Add section dividers.
- Improve storyline consistency.
- Final polish for executive readiness.
